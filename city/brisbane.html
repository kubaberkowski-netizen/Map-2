Offbeat & storied places in Brisbane

📜 Deep history · 7

  • Allison Engine Testing StandsEagle Farm
    Concrete-and-brick blast stands where WWII fighter engines were run flat-out, now the last relics of Brisbane's wartime airfield.
  • Cloudland Memorial ArchBowen Hills
    A neon-lit parabolic arch and ceramic dancefloor memorialising the beloved Cloudland Ballroom, controversially demolished in a 1982 midnight raid.
  • Fort Lytton National ParkLytton
    An 1880s pentagonal coastal fort hidden behind a moat and grassy ramparts, built to guard the mouth of the Brisbane River from naval attack.
  • Les BubblesFortitude Valley
    Parisian steakhouse inside the notorious Fitzgerald-Inquiry brothel, casino and bathhouse building.
  • PinkenbaPinkenba
    A quiet working-port riverside suburb on a narrow strip of land between the Brisbane River and the airport, anchored by the century-old Pinkenba Hotel ('The Pink').
  • Sir Charles Kingsford Smith MemorialEagle Farm
    A glass hangar near Brisbane Airport housing the Southern Cross, the Fokker trimotor that made the first trans-Pacific flight to Australia in 1928.
  • St Helena Island National ParkMoreton Bay
    A ruin-covered Moreton Bay island that was Queensland's first penal settlement, once nicknamed 'the hell hole of the Pacific' and now reachable only by tour boat.

🌿 Green & river · 5

  • Banks Street ReserveAlderley
    A tucked-away remnant of Brisbane's lost Three Mile Scrub rainforest, threaded with bush tracks just 5km from the CBD.
  • Cedar Creek FallsCedar Creek
    Emerald rock pools and a tumbling cascade reached by rock-hopping up a creek in the Samford/Mount Glorious foothills.
  • Coochiemudlo IslandCoochiemudlo Island
    A small Moreton Bay island ringed by beaches with a tiny village, reachable by a short passenger ferry or vehicle barge from Victoria Point.
  • Nerima GardensIpswich
    A free Japanese garden of tea house, ornamental lake and boardwalks tucked inside Ipswich's heritage Queens Park.
  • Walkabout Creek Discovery CentreEnoggera Reservoir
    A native-wildlife centre and platypus house on the shore of Enoggera Reservoir, the gateway into D'Aguilar National Park.

🗿 Oddities · 4

  • Empire Revival (former Plaza Theatre)Paddington
    A 50-dealer antiques collective inside a 1929 heritage atmospheric cinema with a painted night-sky ceiling.
  • Golden GondolaNewstead
    Venetian-style covered gondola dining cruises that glide along the Brisbane River from a Newstead dock.
  • Pitch Drop ExperimentSt Lucia
    The world's longest-running laboratory experiment: a blob of pitch dripping through a funnel since 1930, on public view at UQ.
  • Seized Wildlife Display, Brisbane AirportBrisbane Airport
    A glass cabinet of contraband seized from smugglers at the border, including a taxidermied mongoose locked in mid-fight with a cobra.

🏮 Hidden alleys · 2

  • Burnett LaneBrisbane City
    Brisbane's oldest laneway, a former convict exercise yard now strung with tiny street-art doors, cafes and cocktail bars.
  • Winn LaneFortitude Valley
    A hidden Fortitude Valley laneway of street art, boutiques and tiny bars wedged behind the old Shannon's building.

🏛️ Museums · 2

  • Commissariat Store MuseumBrisbane City
    Queensland's oldest occupied building, a convict-built 1829 store on William Street now packed with penal-colony relics.
  • MacArthur Museum BrisbaneBrisbane City
    General Douglas MacArthur's preserved wartime office on the 8th floor of his old Allied HQ, the nerve centre of the South West Pacific.

🍺 Historic pubs · 2

  • Mrs J. RabbitsWoolloongabba
    A 1920s whisky-and-jazz speakeasy reached through a padlocked wardrobe, hidden above Electric Avenue.
  • NetherworldFortitude Valley
    Retro arcade bar with 25 arcade machines, 15 pinball tables, 24 craft taps and an all-vegan diner.

🏊 Lidos & ponds · 1

  • Bunya Crossing ReserveBunya
    A deep river waterhole with an inland beach and rope swings on a gentle bend of the South Pine River.

🍽️ Institutions · 1

  • Eat Street NorthshoreHamilton
    A riverfront street-food market built from about 180 repurposed shipping containers, open Friday to Sunday.

🎨 Street art · 1

  • Fish LaneSouth Brisbane
    A 450-metre open-air gallery of murals, sculpture and light installations woven through South Brisbane's bars and restaurants.

☕ Coffee · 1

  • Fonzie Abbott Roastery, Brewery & DistilleryAlbion
    A back-street Albion warehouse that roasts coffee, brews beer and distils gin and vodka under one roof.

⚓ Maritime · 1

  • Shorncliffe PierShorncliffe
    At 351.5 m, Brisbane's longest historic timber pier reaches deep into Bramble Bay - a white-railed colonial promenade rebuilt in 2016 after marine borers ate the original.

🪦 Death & burial · 1

  • Toowong Cemetery (Spook Hill)Toowong
    Queensland's largest cemetery, 44 hectares of Victorian monuments, home to the city's most famous 'gravity hill' urban legend.

🎸 Music lore · 1

  • Underground Opera at the Spring Hill ReservoirSpring Hill
    Opera performed six metres underground inside an 1870s decommissioned water reservoir in Wickham Park.
