Offbeat & storied places in Johannesburg

📜 Deep history · 8

  • Johannesburg Postal TunnelsJohannesburg CBD
    A 1930s concrete tunnel network under the CBD that once conveyed 33,000 mailbags a week between the Jeppe Street Post Office and Park Station.
  • Melville Koppies Nature ReserveMelville
    Inner-city quartzite ridge that is a Johannesburg heritage site, holding a 500-year-old Iron Age iron-smelting furnace.
  • NorthwardsParktown
    A 1904 Herbert Baker mansion built for Randlord John Dale Lace and his scandal-courting socialite wife Jose, now a heritage venue for monthly concerts and tours.
  • Rand ClubMarshalltown
    Johannesburg's grand 1880s gentlemen's club on Loveday Street, whose Buckland Library holds what is claimed to be the world's largest collection of Punch cartoons.
  • Satyagraha HouseOrchards
    Gandhi's former Johannesburg home, 'The Kraal,' designed by his friend Hermann Kallenbach and now a small museum and guesthouse.
  • Sterkfontein CavesCradle of Humankind
    Limestone caves ~40 km NW of the city that yielded 'Mrs Ples' and 'Little Foot' — among the richest hominid-fossil sites on Earth.
  • Troyeville HotelTroyeville
    A 1939 Johannesburg original on the bohemian eastern edge, a haunt of artists and Portuguese cooks.
  • Westcliff StairsWestcliff
    Joburg's 'stairway to heaven' — about 210 hand-laid quartzite steps climbing the Westcliff Ridge, built as Depression-era relief work.

🍽️ Institutions · 5

  • 44 StanleyMilpark
    A 1930s light-industrial complex turned leafy precinct of boutiques, cafes and an indie cinema.
  • Dinner in the Sky JohannesburgBryanston
    A dining table for 22 guests hoisted dozens of metres into the air by crane, run as a regular weekend event on the grounds of St Stithians College.
  • Rosebank Sunday MarketRosebank
    A 200-plus-stall Sunday market spread across the rooftop of Rosebank Mall.
  • The Playground MarketBraamfontein
    A weekend food-and-design market in the old Neighbourgoods space on Braamfontein's Juta Street.
  • Yeoville (Congolese) MarketYeoville
    A pan-African market along Rockey Street where the continent's diaspora trades spices, produce and crafts.

🗿 Oddities · 4

  • 27 BoxesMelville
    South Africa's first shipping-container shopping village, built on a disused Melville park.
  • Honeydew MazesZandspruit (Honeydew)
    A farm on the city's NW edge whose giant maze switches with the seasons — a living maize maze early in the year, a reed-walled Elemental Maze in winter and spring.
  • Museum of Man and ScienceJohannesburg CBD
    A traditional muti shop near Diagonal Street whose ceiling and walls are a cabinet of curiosities of dried herbs, skins, horns and bones.
  • Victoria YardsLorentzville
    An old industrial site in Lorentzville reborn as an open-studio creative precinct with a First-Sunday market.

🏛️ Museums · 4

  • Keyes Art MileRosebank
    A Rosebank gallery strip anchoring contemporary African art, home to Africa's oldest commercial gallery.
  • Lindfield Victorian House MuseumAuckland Park
    A lived-in Victorian house crammed with curiosities, where the owner gives tours in period dress and lets you try on a cage crinoline.
  • Maropeng Visitor CentreCradle of Humankind
    A grass-covered tumulus mound in the veld that houses the official human-origins museum of the Cradle of Humankind.
  • Wits Anglo American Digital DomeBraamfontein
    The 1960 Johannesburg Planetarium reborn in 2025 as an 8K full-dome immersive theatre — the largest digital dome in the southern hemisphere.

🌿 Green & river · 3

  • Delta Park & Florence Bloom Bird SanctuaryVictory Park
    Joburg's 104-hectare green lung, home to the oldest bird sanctuary in Gauteng with 250+ recorded species.
  • Nirox Sculpture ParkKromdraai
    Thirty hectares of landscaped lawns and wetlands in the Cradle, studded with monumental sculpture from artists worldwide.
  • The Wilds Nature ReserveHoughton
    A 16-hectare inner-city koppie garden, once a no-go zone, reborn with 100+ laser-cut animal sculptures.

🎨 Street art · 2

  • Arts on Main (Maboneng)Maboneng
    The first building of the Maboneng arts district, surrounded by inner-city murals and a Sunday market.
  • Orlando TowersSoweto
    Two decommissioned power-station cooling towers in Soweto, one painted as Africa's largest mural, now an adventure rig for bungee and BASE jumps.

📚 Bookshops · 1

  • Collectors TreasuryJohannesburg CBD
    An eight-storey CBD warehouse said to be the largest used and rare bookstore in Africa, with around two million items stacked into every corner.

🏰 Follies · 1

  • MontecasinoFourways
    A casino and entertainment complex built as a fake ancient Tuscan village, complete with a painted day-to-night 'sky' ceiling and an adjoining bird park.

🌆 Rooftop views · 1

  • Munro Drive ViewpointHoughton
    A 1918 jacaranda-lined pass through Linksfield Ridge with a free panoramic lookout over the northern suburbs.

🧱 Brutalism · 1

  • Ponte City TowerBerea
    Africa's first cylindrical skyscraper — a 54-storey concrete cylinder in Berea wrapped around a hollow core, long a symbol of inner-city Joburg's rise and fall.
