Offbeat & storied places in Rabat

📜 Deep history · 5

  • Borj Adoumoue (Bastion des Larmes)Bou Regreg riverfront, Salé
    The 'Bastion of Tears' on the Salé bank, a 17th-century fort guarding the river mouth in the age of the Sallee Rover corsairs and their human cargo.
  • Borj Sidi MakhloufMedina
    A rounded bastion on the river cliff, named for a Jewish-born marabout
  • Hassan TowerTour Hassan / Yacoub al-Mansour Esplanade
    The stump of a 12th-century minaret that was meant to crown the largest mosque in the western Islamic world, abandoned mid-build and ringed by a field of broken columns.
  • Kasbah des GnaouaBou Regreg right bank, Salé
    The only kasbah on the Salé side of the river, raised by Moulay Ismail and garrisoned by his Black Guard to watch the corsair coast.
  • Rue des ConsulsMedina of Rabat
    The medina's arcaded carpet street, where foreign consuls were once required to live so they could ransom captives from the Bou Regreg corsairs.

🪦 Death & burial · 4

  • El Alou CemeteryBelow the Kasbah des Oudayas
    Acres of leaning white headstones spilling from the kasbah's red walls down to the Atlantic, where Edith Wharton found 'infinite sadness' under a fading sky.
  • Mausoleum of Mohammed VYacoub al-Mansour Esplanade (opposite the Hassan Tower)
    A white onyx-and-marble royal tomb facing the Hassan Tower across the column field, its gates flanked by mounted royal guards in scarlet.
  • Rabat European CemeteryAgdal
    The colonial dead of Rabat, with French war graves and a Russian chapel beside
  • Sidi Ben Achir CemeteryBab Lamrissa, Salé seafront
    A vast white necropolis tumbling toward the Atlantic around the tomb of a 14th-century healer-saint, beside whom the sick and the mad once came to sleep for a cure.

🛕 Faith & ritual · 4

  • El Atika (Oudayas) MosqueKasbah des Oudayas
    The oldest mosque in Rabat — an Almohad foundation of 1150 later rebuilt by an English corsair turned Muslim convert.
  • Holy Resurrection Russian Orthodox ChurchMedina
    Africa's oldest open Russian church, built by White emigres in 1932
  • Lalla Soukaina MosqueSouissi
    A 1980s royal mosque of fountains, gardens and a retractable roof
  • Zaouia of Sidi Abdellah ben HassounSalé Medina
    The shrine of Salé's patron saint, where on the eve of the Prophet's birthday a torchlit crowd carries man-high lanterns of carved, gilded wax to be left for a year.

🌿 Green & river · 3

  • Andalusian Gardens of the OudayasKasbah des Oudayas
    A walled Moorish garden of roses and fruit trees, planted by the French inside a 12th-century citadel and modelled on the Alhambra and Seville.
  • Jardin d'Essais BotaniquesAgdal district
    A 1914 colonial test garden laid out on the old royal Agdal orchards, designed to acclimatise exotic plants and to show off French botanical prestige.
  • Rabat Zoo (Atlas Lion Bloodline)Hay Riad
    The garden that keeps the bloodline of the lion extinct in the wild

🏙️ Art Deco · 3

  • Avenue Mohammed V (Ville Nouvelle)Ville Nouvelle, between Bab el-Had and the Hassan Tower quarter
    The grand colonial boulevard once called Cours Lyautey, a parade of white Art Deco and Mauresque civic palaces along Henri Prost's grid.
  • St. Peter's Cathedral (Cathédrale Saint-Pierre)Place Al Joulane (Place du Golan), Ville Nouvelle
    A white Art Deco church with twin pointed towers rising over a downtown square, blending 1920s geometry with Moorish detail.
  • Villa des Arts de RabatHassan
    A blinding-white 1929 Art Deco villa turned contemporary gallery

🏰 Medieval · 3

  • Bab er-RouahAlmohad ramparts, Place An-Nasr
    The 'Gate of the Winds', a monumental Almohad portal whose red stone the Atlantic gales have weathered to grey, now an art gallery.
  • Bab OudaiaKasbah des Oudayas
    The Almohads' monumental kasbah gate, carved with interlacing geometry and a Kufic war-verse — and a rare, almost-hidden frieze of serpents.
  • Madrasa of Abu al-HasanSalé Medina
    A jewel-box Marinid Quranic college beside the Great Mosque, every surface dripping with zellij, carved stucco and cedarwood since the 1340s.

🏛️ Museums · 3

  • Mohammed VI Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art (MMVI)Avenue Moulay Hassan, Ville Nouvelle
    Morocco's first big new museum since independence, a white double-arched palace of modern Moroccan and African art.
  • Museum of History and CivilizationsHassan
    Morocco's archaeology museum, home to the bronze busts dug from Roman Volubilis
  • Oudayas Museum (National Museum of Jewellery)Kasbah des Oudayas
    A pleasure-palace built for Sultan Moulay Ismail inside the kasbah, now Morocco's national jewellery museum after a century as an ethnographic collection.

⚓ Maritime · 2

  • Bab el-MrissaSalé Medina
    A monumental Marinid sea-gate so tall that war-galleys once sailed straight off the Bou Regreg, through its horseshoe arch, into a hidden inner dockyard.
  • Phare de Rabat (Sirat Bastion Lighthouse)Oudayas
    A 1919 lighthouse raised on a bastion built by an English renegade

🏛️ Finance lore · 1

  • Bank Al-Maghrib Currency MuseumHassan
    The central bank's money museum, 30,000 coins from antiquity to the dirham

🌆 Rooftop views · 1

  • Café Maure des OudayasKasbah des Oudayas
    A tiled terrace café on the kasbah's seaward rampart where generations have drunk mint tea over the Bou Regreg, watching Salé across the water.

🏺 Roman London · 1

  • Chellah (Sala Colonia)Bab Zaer, south of the city walls
    A walled Marinid necropolis built over a buried Roman town, where storks nest on a ruined minaret above a pool of supposedly sacred eels.

🌍 Diaspora London · 1

  • Rabbi Shalom Zaoui SynagogueMellah, Medina of Rabat
    A red-courtyard synagogue in Rabat's old Jewish quarter, built into the house of a revered 19th-century rabbi against the medina's Andalusian wall.
