  • Karahori ShotengaiTamatsukuri
    War-spared shopping lane of machiya rowhouses turned cafes
  • NakazakichoKita-ku
    Wooden tenement quarter that dodged the firebombs, now full of folk-house cafes
  • Orange Street (Tachibana-dori)Horie
    Old furniture-makers' street reborn as a low-slung boutique strip
  • Osaka City Central Public HallNakanoshima

🛕 Faith & ritual · 5

  • Hozenji and the Mizukake FudoNamba
    Moss-furred deity drenched daily with water by hopeful hands
  • Katsuo-ji TempleMinoo
    Mountain temple of winning luck, carpeted in thousands of stubby daruma dolls
  • Namba Yasaka JinjaNamba
    Shrine with a 12-metre lion's head that swallows bad luck whole
  • Shitennoji Turtle PondTennoji
    Japan's oldest state temple, ringed by a pond of abandoned pet turtles
  • Sumiyoshi TaishaSumiyoshi-ku
    Ancient sea-god shrine with a near-vertical drum bridge and a fortune-telling stone
