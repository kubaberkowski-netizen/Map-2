🛕 Faith & ritual · 5

  • Gamle Aker kirkeSt. Hanshaugen
    A squat Romanesque basilica of Ekeberg limestone that is the oldest standing building in Oslo, raised on the old Aker farm hill before the city below it existed.
  • Gol Stave ChurchNorsk Folkemuseum, Bygdøy
    A 12th-century stave church rescued from demolition and re-raised on Bygdøy, its tarred timbers smelling of the Norwegian Middle Ages.
  • Hovedøya Abbey ruinsHovedøya
    The roofless walls of a 1147 Cistercian abbey on a fjord island, founded by English monks and later quarried to build Akershus Fortress.
  • Kampen kirkeKampen
    A neo-Gothic brick church crowning the hilltop village of old wooden houses at Kampen, on a site cleared by fire.
  • St. Margaret's Church ruinsMaridalen
    The lichen-grey shell of a 13th-century stone church at the head of lake Maridalsvannet, backdrop to an open-air play staged almost every summer since 1974.

  • Gressholmen seaplane harbourGressholmen
    Oslo's first real airport was here — a fjord island where flying boats splashed down and passengers were rowed ashore.

🪦 Death & burial · 3

  • Sofienbergparken (gamle jødiske gravlund)Sofienberg, Grünerløkka
    A popular Grünerløkka park laid out over a vast old cemetery, where an iron-fenced corner preserves Norway's oldest Jewish burial ground, untouched out of respect for Jewish law.
  • Vår Frelsers gravlund (Æreslunden)St. Hanshaugen / Gamle Aker
    Norway's most famous cemetery, where Ibsen, Munch and Bjørnson lie in the Æreslunden grove of honour a short walk from the medieval Aker church.
  • Villa Grande (HL-senteret)Bygdøy
    The Nazi collaborator Vidkun Quisling's grandiose Bygdøy mansion, now Norway's Holocaust and minorities study centre.

🌆 Rooftop views · 2

  • Ekeberg "Scream" viewpointEkeberg
    The forested ledge above the fjord where Edvard Munch felt the "infinite scream of nature" that became his most famous painting.
  • TryvannstårnetTryvann / Holmenkollen
    Oslo's 118-metre TV tower on the city's highest ridge, its panorama deck shuttered since 2005 but bought in 2025 to be reborn as a café.
