📜 Deep history · 6

  • Citadelle de VaubanBois de Boulogne
    Vauban's five-pointed star fort, the 'Queen of Citadels', still army-held and ringed by woods.
  • Colonne de la DeesseGrand'Place
    Bronze goddess clutching a cannon fuse, raised to a siege that refused to surrender
  • Le TripostalCentre / Euralille
    1950s mail-sorting depot saved from the wrecking ball, now three floors of art
  • Porte de ParisCentre
    A triumphal arch grafted onto a city gate, flattering Louis XIV for taking the town in 1667.
  • Rang de BeauregardPlace du Theatre
    1680s row of fourteen matched houses still pocked with siege cannonballs
  • Vieille BourseCentre
    A 1653 Flemish stock exchange of 24 houses round a courtyard, now a bouquinistes' den.

🛕 Faith & ritual · 3

  • Eglise Saint-MauriceCentre
    A rare Flemish hall-church, five aisles of equal height built on marshy ground over 500 years.
  • Eglise Sainte-Marie-MadeleineVieux-Lille
    Domed rotunda church, deconsecrated, Lille's only round nave
  • Notre-Dame-de-la-TreilleVieux-Lille
    A neo-Gothic cathedral begun 1854, finally capped in 1999 by a glowing veil of thin marble.

🏛️ Museums · 3

  • Hospice ComtesseVieux-Lille
    A 1237 countess's hospital, its sick ward now hung with Flemish art and Delft tiles.
  • Maison Natale de Charles de GaulleSaint-Andre
    The bourgeois townhouse where de Gaulle was born in 1890, kept as his family knew it.
  • Palais des Beaux-ArtsCentre
    One of France's biggest art museums, with Goya witches and 3D relief maps of fortified towns.

🏰 Medieval · 3

  • Palais RihourRihour
    Flamboyant Gothic fragment of the Burgundian dukes' lost palace, now the tourist office
  • Porte de GandVieux-Lille
    A 1620 Spanish-era gate guarding rue de Gand, Lille's row of brick-and-beer estaminets.
  • Rue de la MonnaieVieux-Lille
    The old mint street, a curve of 17th-18th century brick gables in the heart of Vieux-Lille.

🌆 Rooftop views · 2

  • Beffroi de l'Hotel de VilleSaint-Sauveur
    104-metre concrete belfry guarded by the two giants of the city's founding legend
  • Beffroi de la Chambre de CommercePlace du Theatre
    76-metre neo-Flemish belfry whose carillon plays 'P'tit Quinquin' on the quarter-hour

🎬 Pop culture · 2

  • Gare Saint-SauveurSaint-Sauveur
    An 1865 goods station, abandoned, now a free arts venue with bistro and rooftop farm.
  • Maison Folie de WazemmesWazemmes
    A dead textile mill reborn in 2004 as a scrappy arts house with a metal-skinned hall.

🎸 Music lore · 2

  • L'AeronefEuralille
    Cult contemporary-music venue tucked on a walkway inside a shopping mall
  • Maison Folie de MoulinsMoulins
    Listed 18th-century brewery turned cross-disciplinary arts factory

🍺 Historic pubs · 1

  • Estaminet 'T RijselVieux-Lille
    A brick-walled Flemish tavern named for Lille's own Dutch name, all stew and local beer.

🗿 Oddities · 1

  • Maison CoilliotCentre
    Guimard's only northern building: an Art Nouveau facade in green enamelled lava, wedged between neighbours.

🍽️ Institutions · 1

  • Marche de WazemmesWazemmes
    Lille's loudest, most polyglot market, its Sunday sprawl flooding the streets around the hall.

🪦 Death & burial · 1

  • Noble TourSaint-Sauveur
    A lone 1402 Burgundian rampart tower, now a memorial holding ashes from the camps.
