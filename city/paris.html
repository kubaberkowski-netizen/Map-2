🌿 Green & river · 6

  • Coulee verte Rene-Dumont (Promenade Plantee)Viaduc des Arts, av. Daumesnil, 12e (Bastille)
    The world's first elevated park, laid along an old railway viaduct in 1993 and the acknowledged inspiration for New York's High Line.
  • Ménagerie du Jardin des Plantes5e (Jardin des Plantes)
    Born of the Revolution in 1794 partly from animals seized at Versailles, this is one of the oldest zoos in the world, a maze of 19th-century pavilions in a botanical garden.
  • Parc de Belleville20e
    A steep hillside park with one of the best free panoramas over Paris, in an un-touristy quarter
  • Parc des Buttes-Chaumont19e
    A dramatic cliff-and-temple park built on a former gypsum quarry and gibbet site
  • Square du Vert-GalantÎle de la Cité
    A willow-tipped prow of the Cite where Templars burned
  • The Robinier of Square René-Viviani5e (Latin Quarter)
    Propped up on a concrete crutch beside the city's oldest church garden stands the oldest tree in Paris, a false acacia planted in 1601.

🪦 Death & burial · 4

  • Cimetière du Père-Lachaise20e

  • Flame of Liberty (Place Diana)Pont de l'Alma, Place Diana, 16e
    A gilded replica of the Statue of Liberty's torch, never meant as a memorial, became the world's spontaneous shrine to Princess Diana above the tunnel where she died.
  • Les Catacombes de Paris14e
    The bones of six million Parisians stacked through former quarry tunnels beneath the city
  • Tombe de Victor NoirPere-Lachaise, division 92, 20e
    A bronze of a journalist shot dead in 1870, sculpted lying as he fell, now rubbed shiny in three places by visitors chasing a fertility legend.
