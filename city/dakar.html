📜 Deep history · 9

  • Gare de DakarPlateau
    The grand 1914 terminus at the head of the old Dakar-Niger railway, mothballed for a decade and reborn as the TER station.
  • Hôtel de Ville de DakarDakar-Plateau
    The city hall of an empire that called its citizens French
  • La MedinaMedina
    Dakar's African quarter, laid out in 1914 when a plague panic gave the colonial state its pretext to expel Africans from the Plateau.
  • Marche KermelPlateau
    A round wrought-iron colonial market hall of 1860 that burned to the ground in 1993 and was rebuilt, identically, with European money.
  • Marche Sandaga (site)Plateau
    For ninety years Dakar's great Sudanese-style market hall stood here, until it was bulldozed in 2021 amid an architects' outcry.
  • Monument de la Renaissance africaineOuakam / Colline des Mamelles
    A 49-metre bronze family striding off a volcanic hill — taller than the Statue of Liberty, conceived by a president and built by North Koreans.
  • Palais de Justice de DakarRebeuss
    The Moorish colonial courthouse where independence was tried
  • Place de l'IndépendanceDakar-Plateau
    The independence square that used to honour a colonial enforcer
  • RufisqueRufisque, east of Dakar
    A faded colonial peanut port east of Dakar — one of Senegal's 'Four Communes' whose Africans won full French citizenship a century before independence.

🛕 Faith & ritual · 6

  • Cathedrale du Souvenir AfricainPlateau
    A 1930s cathedral built as a memorial to Africa's WWI dead, fusing Sudanese towers and Byzantine domes with caryatids carved as Peul women.
  • Église Saint-Charles-BorroméeÎle de Gorée
    One of Senegal's oldest churches, rebuilt by Gorée's signares on the ashes of a chapel the English torched on Christmas Eve 1799.
  • Grande Mosquee de DakarMedina
    The Medina's landmark mosque, its 67-metre minaret modelled on Casablanca and inaugurated by a king of Morocco and a poet-president.
  • Mausolée de Seydina Limamou LayeYoff — Layène quarter, on the beach
    A seaside tomb with sand floors in the fishing village of Yoff, holding the founder of the Layène Sufi order who declared himself the Mahdi on this coast.
  • Mosquée de GoréeÎle de Gorée
    A small whitewashed mosque of black basalt stone tucked at the foot of the Castel cliff — among the oldest stone mosques in Senegal.
  • Mosquée de la DivinitéOuakam, in the cove below the cliffs
    A white twin-minaret mosque wedged into an Ouakam cove at the water's edge, said to have been revealed in a dream and built almost entirely by hand.

🏛️ Museums · 5

  • Fort d'Estrées (Musée historique du Sénégal)Île de Gorée
    A horseshoe-shaped seafront citadel on Gorée's north tip, now IFAN's national history museum from prehistory to independence.
  • Galerie Nationale d'Art du SénégalDakar-Plateau
    West Africa's first national art gallery, small and quietly essential
  • IFAN Musee Theodore Monod d'Art africainPlateau
    A 1930s neo-Sudanese palace on Place Soweto holding one of West Africa's greatest collections of masks and African art.
  • Musée de la Femme Henriette-BathilyFann Résidence
    From Goree's Signares to today's activists, one museum
  • Musee des Civilisations noiresPlateau
    A vast 2018 pan-African museum shaped like a Casamance impluvium hut, realising a dream Senghor first voiced in 1966.

⚓ Maritime · 3

  • Phare des MamellesOuakam / Les Mamelles
    An 1864 lighthouse perched on the taller of Dakar's two volcanic 'breast' hills, its Fresnel lens still floating in mercury 120 m above the Atlantic.
  • Plage de SoumbediouneFann Hock
    Lebou fishing beach where the catch becomes craft stalls by noon
  • Plage et village de pêcheurs d'OuakamOuakam — at the foot of the Mamelles cliffs
    A working Lebou fishing beach under the Mamelles cliffs, where pirogues land beneath the Renaissance Monument and the surf rolls into Secret Spot.

🎸 Music lore · 2

  • Grand Théâtre National du SénégalMédina
    The China-gifted concert hall at the heart of Dakar
  • Théâtre National Daniel SoranoDakar-Plateau
    Senegal's grandest stage, named for a Frenchman who loved Africa

🌿 Green & river · 2

  • Îles de la Madeleine (Îlot Sarpan)Îles de la Madeleine, off Soumbédioune
    A wind-stunted volcanic islet of dwarf baobabs and tropicbird colonies — billed as the smallest national park in the world, reached by pirogue from Soumbédioune.
  • Parc forestier et zoologique de HannHann Bel-Air
    A century-old colonial botanical garden and zoo on Hann Bay, a green lung of arboretum, classified forest and crocodile pools inside the city.

🌆 Rooftop views · 2

  • Le CastelÎle de Gorée
    Gorée's basalt high point, crowned with rusting coastal guns and a sail-shaped slave memorial, looking back across the bay to Dakar.
  • Pointe des AlmadiesAlmadies — the western tip of the Cap-Vert peninsula
    The westernmost point of mainland Africa and of the whole Afro-Eurasian landmass, where fishermen grill the morning's catch in shacks above the rocks.

🪦 Death & burial · 1

  • Cimetière Militaire de ThiaroyeThiaroye
    Where France shot its own soldiers for asking to be paid

🏊 Lidos & ponds · 1

  • Île de NgorOff Ngor village, reached by pirogue
    A car-free islet 400 m offshore, reached by painted pirogue, whose right-hand reef break made Dakar the opening scene of surfing's most famous film.

🗿 Oddities · 1

  • Lac Rose (Lac Retba)Lac Retba, north-east of Dakar
    A strawberry-pink salt lake saltier than the sea, worked by waist-deep harvesters — and for two decades the finish line of the Paris–Dakar Rally.

🌍 Diaspora London · 1

  • Maison des EsclavesÎle de Gorée
    A coral-pink slave house whose narrow seaward doorway became the world's emblem of the Middle Passage — the 'Door of No Return'.
