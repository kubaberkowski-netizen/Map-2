📜 Deep history · 10

  • Bab Bou JeloudFes el-Bali
    The famous blue gate, only a century old, blue on one face and green on the other.
  • Bab DekkakinFes el-Jdid
    The towering ceremonial gate of the Mechouar, stage for royal parades and grisly displays.
  • Bab SemmarineFes el-Jdid
    The farriers' gate into Fes el-Jdid, once the threshold of the Jewish Mellah.
  • Dar al-MakinaFes el-Jdid
    A sultan's ruined arms factory of the 1880s, built by Italian engineers to make rifles.
  • Dar GlaouiFes el-Bali
    A crumbling palace of the Glaoui warlords, still part-lived-in, fading room by gilded room.
  • Funduq al-TetwaniyyinFes el-Bali
    One of the city's oldest merchant caravanserais, built for traders from Tetouan in the north.
  • Funduq SaghaFes el-Bali
    The goldsmiths' inn of 1711, an Alaouite-era trading court near the coppersmiths' square.
  • Kasbah An-NouarFes el-Bali
    A walled-off citadel quarter of Saharan settlers, self-governed for centuries by twelve elders.
  • Maristan of Sidi FrejFes el-Bali
    One of the world's first psychiatric hospitals, founded 1286, where music and water treated the mad.
  • University of al-QarawiyyinFes el-Bali
    Founded by a woman in 859, often called the oldest university still running, with a library only reopened in 2016.

🏰 Medieval · 4

  • Al-Attarine MadrasaSouk al-Attarine
    The perfumers' college, a jewel-box of zellij tucked behind the spice souk.
  • Bou Inania MadrasaTala'a Kebira
    A Marinid sultan's college so grand it doubled as a Friday mosque, all cedar and zellij.
  • Cherratine MadrasaFes el-Bali
    The rope-makers' college, plainer and later than the rest, built to lodge far-flung students.
  • Sahrij MadrasaAndalusian Quarter, Fes el-Bali
    A Marinid college named for the reflecting pool at its heart, with some of Morocco's earliest zellij.

🛕 Faith & ritual · 4

  • Chrabliyine MosqueTala'a Kebira, Fes el-Bali
    The slipper-makers' mosque, its Marinid minaret one of the finest on the main medina street.
  • Ibn Danan SynagogueMellah
    A 17th-century synagogue behind an unmarked door, its rabbis drawn from one family for 150 years.
  • Mosque of the AndalusiansAdwat al-Andalus
    Founded the same year as al-Qarawiyyin, anchor of the quarter built by refugees from Spain.
  • Zaouia of Moulay Idriss IIFes el-Bali
    The tomb of the city's founder, ringed by wooden bars that once barred outsiders and donkeys.

🏛️ Museums · 3

  • Al Batha Museum (Dar Batha)Bab Bou Jeloud
    A sultan's Hispano-Moorish palace turned museum, its Andalusian garden grown wild and shady.
  • Borj Nord Arms MuseumNorth hills
    A 16th-century Saadian fort modelled on Portuguese bastions, now stuffed with five centuries of weaponry.
  • Nejjarine Funduq and FountainPlace Nejjarine
    A 1711 caravanserai where merchants once stabled mules, now a museum of woodwork beside a tiled fountain.

🗿 Oddities · 3

  • Chouara TanneryFes el-Bali
    Medieval dye-pits worked the same way for a thousand years, viewed from a leather shop's roof.
  • Dar al-Magana Water ClockTala'a Kebira
    A 14th-century hydraulic clock whose mechanism nobody alive fully understands.
  • Place Seffarine and Saffarin MadrasaFes el-Bali
    A square that rings all day with coppersmiths' hammers, beside the city's first madrasa.

🪦 Death & burial · 2

  • Marinid TombsNorth hills
    Ruined royal mausolea on a bare hill, the city's best sunset and a dynasty's forgotten dead.
  • Mellah Jewish CemeteryMellah
    A field of blinding whitewashed tombs sloping toward the river, including a teenage martyr's grave.

🌆 Rooftop views · 1

  • Borj SudSouth of Fes el-Bali
    A Saadian hilltop fort with the city's broadest view across the whole medina basin.

🌿 Green & river · 1

  • Jnan Sbil GardensBetween the medinas
    A royal pleasure garden, opened to the city, where old waterwheels still turn beside the river.
