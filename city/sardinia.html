🪦 Death & burial · 12

  • Aiodda Tomba dei GigantiNurallao, Sarcidano
    A megalithic Nuragic burial gallery with a broken arched stele, hidden on a hillside in the sacred Sarcidano uplands near Nurallao.
  • Domus de Janas La RoccaSedini (Anglona, Sassari)
    Dubbed the 'cathedral of the domus de janas' — a tomb the size of a three-storey house carved into a boulder in the middle of the village.
  • Imbertighe Tomba dei GigantiBorore, Marghine
    A giants' tomb famed for its towering carved central stele, one of the finest such monuments known in Sardinia, near Borore.
  • Madau Tombe dei GigantiFonni, Barbagia di Ollolai
    A cluster of four giants' tombs on a plateau in the Madau valley, high on the slopes of Gennargentu below the Corr'e Boi pass.
  • Necropolis of Anghelu RujuAlghero (Sassari)
    The largest rock-cut 'fairy house' necropolis of pre-Nuragic Sardinia, found by accident in a winery's fields.
  • Necropolis of MontessuVillaperuccio (Sulcis, South Sardinia)
    Over forty rock-cut tombs ranged across a natural trachyte amphitheatre on the flank of a 'silent' hill.
  • Necropolis of Sant'Andrea PriuBonorva (Sassari)
    A labyrinthine rock-cut necropolis whose 'Tomb of the Chief' has some 18 chambers and was later turned into a frescoed church.
  • Osono Tomba dei GigantiTriei, Ogliastra
    A Nuragic giants' tomb discovered in the 1990s, set among holm-oak woodland in the Osono locality near Triei in Ogliastra.
  • Sa Domu 'e s'Orcu Tomba dei Giganti (Siddi)Siddi, Marmilla
    One of Sardinia's best-preserved giants' tombs, 'the House of the Ogre', built of finely worked basalt overlooking the Giara di Siddi.
  • Tomba dei Giganti di Coddu VecchiuArzachena
    A Giants' Tomb whose 4-metre arched granite stele is the tallest known in Sardinia.
  • Tomba dei Giganti di Li LolghiArzachena
    A long Giants' Tomb with a sweeping slab exedra and a tall arched central stele.
  • Tomba dei Giganti di S'Ena 'e ThomesDorgali
    An isolated, remarkably intact Giants' Tomb with a near-4-metre granite stele on the road to Lula.

📜 Deep history · 11

  • Gairo VecchioGairo, Ogliastra
    A flood-condemned ghost village on the slopes of Monte Trunconi, emptied after 1951 and finally abandoned in the 1960s.
  • Laveria BrasseyNaracauli / Ingurtosu, Arbus, Costa Verde
    The skeletal ruin of a vast seafront ore-washing plant on the Costa Verde, built to treat zinc blende from the Ingurtosu mines.
  • LolloveNuoro
    A tiny near-ghost medieval village minutes from Nuoro, said to be cursed never to grow and never to die.
  • Mont'e Prama Archaeological AreaCabras
    The Sinis-peninsula field where the giant Nuragic stone warriors, archers and boxers were unearthed.
  • Nuraghe LosaAbbasanta
    One of Sardinia's best-preserved nuraghi: a complex basalt tower-fortress on the Abbasanta plateau.
  • Nuraghe NolzaMeana Sardo
    A towering multi-tower nuraghe crowning a schist plateau in the mountainous heart of Barbagia.
  • Pranu Mutteddu Archaeological ParkGoni (South Sardinia)
    Sardinia's densest field of standing stones — some sixty menhirs, megalithic tombs and a domus de janas ringed by stone circles.
  • RebeccuBonorva, Sassari
    A near-deserted medieval hamlet on a rocky spur above the Santa Lucia plain, bound by a curse that it would never exceed thirty houses.
  • Serra Orrios Nuragic VillageDorgali
    A well-preserved Nuragic village of round huts with two megaron temples, hidden in olive groves near Dorgali.
  • Tiscali Nuragic VillageSupramonte, between Dorgali and Oliena
    A Bronze Age village hidden inside a collapsed limestone doline, invisible until you stand at the lip of the cave.
  • Villaggio AsproniGonnesa / Iglesias, Sulcis-Iglesiente
    An abandoned company mining village on Monte San Giovanni, built in the 1870s and left to ruin when the pits closed.

⚓ Maritime · 2

  • Isola dell'AsinaraPorto Torres, Sassari
    A former 'devil's island' prison off Sardinia's north-west tip, now a national park - this pin marks the Cala Reale anchorage.
  • Isola di TavolaraPorto San Paolo / Olbia, Gallura
    A sheer limestone island off Olbia styled 'the world's smallest kingdom' - this pin marks the Spalmatore di Terra settlement.

🧱 Brutalism · 1

  • CarboniaSulcis-Iglesiente
    A rationalist 'citta di fondazione' raised from nothing in 16 months to mine coal for Mussolini's autarky, inaugurated in 1938.

🏰 Medieval · 1

  • Castello di AcquafreddaSiliqua, Sud Sardegna
    A ruined 13th-century hilltop castle on a volcanic plug near Siliqua, tied to Dante's Count Ugolino della Gherardesca.

🛕 Faith & ritual · 1

  • Pozzo Sacro di Santa CristinaPaulilatino (Oristano)
    A Nuragic sacred well whose precision-cut stepped staircase descends to a tholos chamber over a spring of water.

🗿 Oddities · 1

  • Roccia dell'ElefanteCastelsardo (Sassari)
    A wind-sculpted trachyte boulder that looks like a crouching elephant, with prehistoric tombs carved into its body.

🌿 Green & river · 1

  • S'ArchittuSanta Caterina di Pittinuri, Cuglieri
    A 15 m limestone sea-arch on Sardinia's west coast, the surviving vault of a collapsed coastal cave and a celebrated cliff-diving spot.
