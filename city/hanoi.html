📜 Deep history · 8

  • Ancient House at 87 Ma MayOld Quarter
    A restored tube house showing how Old Quarter merchants squeezed a life into a sliver of street.
  • B-52 Wreckage, Huu Tiep LakeNgoc Ha
    The tail and undercarriage of a downed bomber still jut from a pond between houses.
  • Hang Ma StreetOld Quarter
    The lane of paper offerings, where you can buy a cardboard motorbike to burn for the dead.
  • Hoa Lo PrisonHoan Kiem
    A French colonial prison, later the wry-named Hanoi Hilton, kept as a fragment of museum.
  • Lan Ong Herb StreetOld Quarter
    A whole lane of traditional medicine shops where the air carries roots, bark and dried herbs.
  • Long Bien BridgeLong Bien
    A skeletal French iron bridge, bombed and patched, that still carries trains over the Red River.
  • Metropole Wartime BunkerHoan Kiem
    A concrete air-raid shelter rediscovered beneath a grand hotel, where Joan Baez once sheltered.
  • O Quan Chuong GateOld Quarter
    The single surviving gate of the old earthen city, a squat watchtower swallowed by traffic.

🛕 Faith & ritual · 6

  • Bach Ma TempleOld Quarter
    Hanoi's oldest temple, dim with incense, founded on a legend of a guiding white horse.
  • Dong Lac Communal HouseOld Quarter
    A tiny upstairs guild shrine to silk traders, hidden above the shops of the old silk street.
  • Hai Ba Trung Temple, Dong NhanDong Nhan
    A temple to two rebel queens, holding red-faced statues said to have floated up the river.
  • Quan Thanh TempleTruc Bach
    A Taoist temple guarding the north, built around a near-four-metre black bronze god.
  • Than Quang Pagoda, Ngu XaTruc Bach
    A bronze-casters' pagoda by Truc Bach Lake, home to a colossal cast Buddha.
  • Tran Quoc PagodaWest Lake
    Hanoi's oldest pagoda on a teardrop islet, a red stupa rising straight from West Lake.

☕ Coffee · 3

  • Cafe GiangOld Quarter
    The cramped alley cafe where egg coffee was invented in the lean years of 1946.
  • Cafe LamOld Quarter
    A dim 1950s coffee house hung with paintings traded by artists who couldn't pay their tab.
  • Hidden Gem CafeOld Quarter
    A coffee shop built almost entirely from salvaged junk, down a motorbike-hushed lane.

🌆 Rooftop views · 1

  • Cafe Pho CoOld Quarter
    A rooftop coffee hideout reached by walking through a silk shop and up a dim spiral stair.

🎨 Street art · 1

  • Hanoi Ceramic Mosaic MuralRed River Dyke
    Kilometres of dyke wall tiled into a record-breaking ceramic mosaic for the city's millennium.

🗿 Oddities · 1

  • Train StreetPhung Hung
    A residential rail corridor where cafes pull in their chairs minutes before a train scrapes past.
