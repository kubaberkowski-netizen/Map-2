🛕 Faith & ritual · 16

  • Agios Athanasios Churchcity centre (Egnatia / Sokratous)
    An early-19th-century post-Byzantine, three-aisled wooden-roofed basilica, one of the oldest surviving of its kind in the city.
  • Church of Saint Catherine (Agia Aikaterini)Ano Poli
    A graceful late-Byzantine brick church in the western Upper Town, its outer walls patterned in decorative brickwork.
  • Church of Saint Nicholas Orphanos (Agios Nikolaos Orfanos)Ano Poli
    A modest garden-hidden church near the eastern wall whose interior is sheathed in some of the finest Palaiologan frescoes anywhere.
  • Church of Saint Panteleimon (Agios Panteleimon)Ano Poli
    A compact late-13th/early-14th-century Byzantine church near the Rotunda and Kamara, of the elegant cross-in-square type.
  • Church of the Prophet Elijah (Profitis Ilias)Ano Poli
    A tall, finely domed late-14th-century church of the Athonite type, built over a palace ruined in the Zealot revolt.
  • Church of the Saviour (Sotirakis)Kamara / city centre
    The city's smallest Byzantine church, affectionately called 'Sotirakis' (little Sotiris); a tiny 14th-century domed chapel that was never converted to a mosque.
  • Hosios David (Latomou Monastery)Ano Poli
    A tiny Ano Poli chapel guarding a luminous late-antique apse mosaic of a youthful, beardless Christ in glory.
  • Hypapante Church (Ypapanti)Kamara / city centre
    Post-Byzantine church on Egnatia whose 1841 carved iconostasis is crowned by a Cross flanked by dragons alluding to the story of the Prophet Jonah.
  • Nea Panagia Churchcity centre (Gounari / Mitropoleos)
    A 1727 post-Byzantine basilica raised on the ruins of a 12th-century Byzantine monastery, with a gilded Greek-Baroque iconostasis.
  • Panagouda Churchcity centre (Egnatia / P.P. Germanou)
    The 1818 'Panagouda' church, a small-domed basilica with a Pantokrator fresco in its dome and a neoclassical bell tower.
  • Saint George Church, Kalohori, Thessaloniki
    church building in Delta Municipality, Greece
  • Saint Photios Church, Thessaloniki
    church building in Thessaloniki, Greece
  • Vlatadon Monastery (Moni Vlatadon)Ano Poli
    The only surviving Byzantine monastery in Thessaloniki, a working community high in the Upper Town with sweeping views over the city and gulf.
  • Yeni Camii (Old Archaeological Museum)Exoches (Hamidiye)
    A 1902 eclectic mosque built for the city's crypto-Jewish Donmeh community, later its first archaeological museum, now a municipal exhibition hall.
