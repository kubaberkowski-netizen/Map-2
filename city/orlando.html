📜 Deep history · 11

  • Angebilt HotelDowntown Orlando
    An 11-story 1923 Orange Avenue hotel, once Orlando's grandest, now converted to offices and ground-floor bars.
  • Bumby BuildingChurch Street, Orlando
    An 1886 red-brick hardware building on Church Street, one of Orlando's first masonry buildings, now a restaurant and venue block.
  • Ivanhoe VillageIvanhoe Village, Orlando
    Orlando's indie 'Antique Row' along Orange Avenue by Lake Ivanhoe — a lakeside Main Street district of vintage shops, breweries and the art museum.
  • Jack Kerouac HouseCollege Park
    The tin-roofed College Park bungalow where Jack Kerouac lived in 1957-58, now a working writers' residency.
  • Old Orlando Railroad DepotChurch Street, Orlando
    The 1889 Victorian train depot at the heart of Church Street Station, now a SunRail stop and ghost-tour landmark.
  • Orange County Regional History CenterDowntown Orlando
    A regional history museum inside a restored 1927 Beaux-Arts courthouse whose old courtrooms once hosted Ted Bundy's trial.
  • Osceola County CourthouseKissimmee
    One of only four Romanesque Revival courthouses still standing in Florida, built 1889
  • Parramore Historic DistrictParramore
    Orlando's oldest African-American neighborhood — a thriving Black enclave of theaters, shops and hotels in segregated Orlando, later cut apart by Interstate 4 and SR 408.
  • Ritz Theater SanfordSanford
    1923 masonry vernacular theater on Sanford's handsome brick Main Street
  • Thornton Park Historic DistrictThornton Park
    A walkable district of brick-lined streets and early-20th-century bungalows just east of Lake Eola, built when Orlando's cattle ranchers and citrus growers put their fortunes into stately homes.
  • Winter Garden Historic DowntownWinter Garden
    A preserved citrus-era brick downtown on Plant Street, where two old railbeds became the West Orange Trail running straight through the main street.

🌿 Green & river · 8

  • Blue Spring State ParkOrange City
    Florida's largest spring on the St. Johns River, winter refuge for hundreds of manatees
  • Harry P. Leu GardensAudubon Park, Orlando
    Nearly 50 acres of botanical garden wrapped around an 1880s farmhouse, with one of the largest camellia collections in the Eastern U.S.
  • Kraft Azalea GardenWinter Park
    A lakeside grove of towering cypress and azaleas on Lake Maitland, anchored by the 'Exedra' — a curved stone monument framing the water.
  • Lake Apopka Wildlife DriveApopka
    11-mile drive through a Superfund cleanup that became a bird sanctuary of surpassing richness
  • Lake Eola ParkDowntown Orlando
    Orlando's signature downtown park, ringed by a lake with swan boats and the fountain that serves as the city's emblem.
  • Mead Botanical GardenWinter Park
    A 47-acre pocket of wild Florida in the middle of Winter Park, threaded with boardwalks, a butterfly garden and a spring-fed creek.
  • Tibet-Butler PreserveWindermere / SW Orange County
    A 440-acre wedge of protected pine flatwoods and bayhead swamp near Lake Tibet, with boardwalks and a nature center minutes from the theme parks.
  • Wekiwa Springs State ParkApopka
    First-magnitude spring pouring 42 million gallons a day into Central Florida's oldest canoe run

🏛️ Museums · 7

  • Charles Hosmer Morse Museum of American ArtWinter Park
    World's largest Tiffany collection, rescued from a burning Long Island estate
  • Mennello Museum of American ArtLoch Haven
    Former private home on Lake Formosa, now guardian of Earl Cunningham's luminous Florida landscapes
  • Moseley House MuseumEatonville
    The second-oldest house in America's oldest Black town — an 1888 wood-frame cottage Zora Neale Hurston used to visit, now a museum of early Eatonville life.
  • Randall Made Knives MuseumOrlando (South Orange Blossom Trail)
    A free 9,000-sq-ft museum tucked behind a working family knife factory that has hand-forged blades since 1938.
  • Rollins Museum of ArtWinter Park
    Florida's only college teaching museum, home to old master paintings on a Spanish moss campus
  • Wells'Built Museum of African American History & CultureParramore
    A Jim Crow-era Black hotel on the chitlin' circuit — host to Jackie Robinson, Ella Fitzgerald and Ray Charles — reborn as a museum in Orlando's Parramore district.
  • Zora Neale Hurston National Museum of Fine ArtsEatonville
    "The Hurston" — Eatonville's museum to the author and folklorist, showcasing art of the African diaspora in the oldest Black-incorporated town in America.

🗿 Oddities · 4

  • Cassadaga HotelCassadaga
    A 1927 Mediterranean Revival hotel where resident mediums still give readings, a few doors from the Spiritualist camp.
  • Celebration Market StreetCelebration
    Disney's utopian new-urbanist town, designed by Philip Johnson and Michael Graves
  • Global Convergence SculptureDowntown Orlando
    A 12-foot solar-lit aluminum globe whose blue panels are printed with native shoal bass that seem to swim laps as the LEDs shift.
  • I-4 Dead ZoneSanford
    A legendary quarter-mile of Interstate 4 near the St Johns River bridge said to be paved over a forgotten 1880s graveyard.

🍽️ Institutions · 3

  • Hollerbach's Willow Tree CafeSanford
    Bavarian schnitzel and oompah bands on Florida's most unexpected cobblestone block
  • The Acre OrlandoCollege Park, Orlando
    A lush one-acre urban garden bar and event yard tucked into College Park, strung with lights for weddings, markets and evening drinks.
  • World's Largest Entertainment McDonald'sInternational Drive
    A three-story, 19,000-sq-ft kitsch McDonald's near International Drive with an arcade, pizza station and a 30-foot Ronald out front.

🛕 Faith & ritual · 2

  • Cassadaga Spiritualist CampCassadaga
    A Spiritualist community of mediums chartered in 1894; its Andrew Jackson Davis bookstore is the camp welcome center.
  • St. Lawrence A.M.E. ChurchEatonville
    Older than the town it stands in — the African Methodist Episcopal congregation founded in 1881 on land donated by Maitland's Lewis Lawrence, six years before Eatonville was incorporated.

🎟️ Cinemas · 2

  • Enzian TheaterMaitland
    Arthouse cinema that opened with Lillian Gish watching D.W. Griffith in 1985
  • Garden TheatreWinter Garden
    1935 downtown cinema resurrected as a live-performance stage on Winter Garden's Plant Street

🌆 Rooftop views · 1

  • Bok Tower GardensLake Wales
    A 205-foot pink-and-grey marble 'Singing Tower' carillon crowning one of peninsular Florida's highest points, ringing out over Edward Bok's landscape garden.

🪦 Death & burial · 1

  • Greenwood CemeteryOrlando
    Orlando's oldest cemetery, founded 1880, full of Victorian monuments, veterans' sections and ghost-tour lore.

🏙️ Art Deco · 1

  • Maitland Art CenterMaitland
    Mayan Revival fantasy compound, a 1937 artist colony now a National Historic Landmark

🏊 Lidos & ponds · 1

  • Rock Springs at Kelly ParkApopka
    A crystal-clear, 68-degree spring run that doubles as a natural lazy river — float it on an inner tube through shaded subtropical woods.

🗄️ Archives · 1

  • The Disney Collection at the Orlando Public LibraryDowntown Orlando
    A Disney park-history archive hidden on the fourth floor of the downtown public library, started before the Magic Kingdom even opened.
