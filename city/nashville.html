📜 Deep history · 18

  • Belle Meade PlantationBelle Meade
    A celebrated thoroughbred stud farm built on enslaved labour — the full story is now told
  • Belmont MansionBelmont-Hillsboro
    Adelicia Acklen's opulent 1850s Italianate villa, the largest house in antebellum Tennessee, now a museum on the Belmont University campus.
  • Bicentennial Capitol MallDowntown
    An outdoor museum of Tennessee history carved into 19 acres of downtown — and it's free
  • Cheekwood Estate and GardensWest Nashville
    Maxwell House coffee built this Georgian mansion; the family sold up and gave it away
  • Five PointsEast Nashville
    The creative crossroads of East Nashville, where five streets meet amid shops, breweries, music venues and the annual Tomato Art Fest.
  • Fort Negley ParkSouth Nashville
    Restored Union stone fort built largely by African American laborers, the largest inland Civil War fort.
  • Germantown Historic DistrictGermantown
    Nashville's oldest neighborhood, an 18-block grid of restored Victorian architecture wrapped around the Nashville Farmers' Market.
  • Hatch Show PrintSoBro / Downtown
    One of America's oldest working letterpress poster shops, hand-printing show posters since 1879 — now a working museum inside the Country Music Hall of Fame.
  • Jubilee HallNorth Nashville
    Fisk students sang their way across Europe to fund this building — and it shows
  • Marathon VillageMarathon / North Gulch
    The brick complex where Marathon Motor Works built the first cars made in the South — now a sprawling adaptive-reuse hub of lofts, shops, studios and a distillery.
  • Old Tennessee State PenitentiaryCockrill Bend
    Castellated 1898 prison on Cockrill Bend, closed since 1992 and a filming location for The Green Mile.
  • Tennessee State CapitolCapitol Hill
    The architect who designed it died before it finished — and is entombed inside its walls
  • The ArcadeDowntown
    Nashville's 1902 glass-roofed shopping arcade — one of the first covered shopping centers in North America, modeled on Milan's Galleria.
  • The HermitageHermitage
    Andrew Jackson's plantation home — the man and the enslaved workers who built his legend
  • Travellers Rest Historic House MuseumOak Hill
    Nashville's oldest house museum, an 1799 plantation home whose interpretation now foregrounds the enslaved people who built and worked it.
  • Two Rivers MansionDonelson
    Elegant 1859 Italianate mansion on former plantation land between the Stones and Cumberland rivers.
  • Union Station HotelDowntown / The Gulch
    A castle-like 1900 Richardsonian Romanesque railway terminal — limestone towers, a stained-glass barrel vault — reborn as a downtown hotel.
  • War Memorial AuditoriumDowntown
    The Grand Ole Opry lived here for four years — the link in the chain between Belcourt and Ryman

🍽️ Institutions · 6

  • Arnold's Country KitchenThe Gulch / SoBro
    The most celebrated meat-and-three in Nashville, a cafeteria-line Southern institution that won a James Beard American Classics award.
  • Corsair DistilleryWedgewood-Houston
    Nashville craft distillery that made buckwheat whiskey before craft was cool
  • GeistGermantown
    A bar and restaurant set inside a converted early-1900s blacksmith shop in historic Germantown, complete with a garden courtyard and outdoor cooking stove.
  • Mas Tacos Por FavorEast Nashville
    A beloved East Nashville taco spot that grew from a vintage Winnebago into a brick-and-mortar fixture famous for its fried-avocado tacos.
  • Silver Sands CafeGermantown / North Nashville
    A family-run soul-food meat-and-three that has fed Nashville for over 70 years, with recipes passed down three generations.
  • The Pfunky GriddleBerry Hill
    A cook-your-own-pancakes breakfast spot where every table has a built-in griddle and you are the chef.

🗿 Oddities · 6

  • Batman BuildingDowntown
    Tennessee's tallest skyscraper has two antennae that look exactly like Batman's ears
  • Fontanel MansionWhites Creek
    Barbara Mandrell's 27,000 sq ft log mega-mansion — one of the world's largest log homes
  • George Boedecker Sculpture GardenBellevue
    Crocs co-founder's gated Bellevue front yard studded with life-size dinosaur and animal sculptures.
  • Hermitage Hotel (Art Deco Men's Room)Downtown
    A grand 1910 hotel whose real cult attraction is downstairs: a lime-green-and-black Art Deco men's room repeatedly voted the best public bathroom in America.
  • Hidden Heart of Music RowMusic Row
    A single heart-shaped stone hidden among thousands of rectangular blocks in a Music Row wall.
  • Nashville ParthenonCentennial Park
    A full-scale concrete replica of the Athens Parthenon, in a city park

🎸 Music lore · 5

  • Bluebird CafeGreen Hills
    A 90-seat strip-mall restaurant where Garth Brooks was discovered in 1987
  • Owen Bradley ParkMusic Row
    The spot where Music Row was born — the Quonset Hut studio once stood here
  • RCA Studio BMusic Row
    The little room on Music Row where Elvis, Dolly and Chet invented a sound
  • Ryman AuditoriumDowntown
    A river captain's guilt trip became country music's holiest hall
  • Station InnThe Gulch
    Nashville's dive-bar cathedral of bluegrass — cash-only, no reserved seats

🍺 Historic pubs · 3

  • ChopperEast Nashville
    A neon, Blade Runner-esque robo-tiki bar in East Nashville with a giant robot hanging from the ceiling and James Beard-recognized birria tacos from the truck parked outside.
  • Dino'sEast Nashville
    East Nashville's oldest dive bar, slinging cheap beer, bourbon and a Bon Appetit-ranked cheeseburger on Gallatin Ave since 1971.
  • Red Phone BoothDowntown
    A 1920s-style speakeasy and cigar lounge entered through a restored red London phone booth, where you dial a secret nightly code to get in.

🏛️ Museums · 3

  • Jefferson Street Sound MuseumNorth Nashville
    A grassroots museum preserving the lost music heritage of Jefferson Street — once the thriving Black R&B district where a young Jimi Hendrix cut his teeth.
  • Johnny Cash MuseumDowntown
    The Man in Black's entire life in one downtown building — world's largest Cash collection
  • Lane Motor MuseumMurfreesboro Pike
    Former Sunbeam bakery packed with rare and eccentric European cars, microcars and oddball prototypes.

🪦 Death & burial · 2

  • Dutchman's CurveWest End / Richland
    Sharp railroad curve west of downtown where America's deadliest train wreck killed about 101 people in 1918.
  • Nashville City CemeteryWedgewood-Houston
    Nashville's oldest boneyard, where Old Glory got her name

🎨 Street art · 2

  • The NationsThe Nations
    A West Nashville industrial neighborhood turned creative quarter, anchored by a 200-foot grain silo wearing a photorealistic mural of a local elder.
  • Wedgewood-Houston (WeHo)Wedgewood-Houston
    A former industrial district just south of downtown reborn as 'WeHo,' Nashville's densest cluster of art galleries, studios and maker spaces.

🎟️ Cinemas · 1

  • Belcourt TheatreHillsboro Village
    Grand Ole Opry played here in the 1930s before the Ryman — the format it uses today was born in this room

🏙️ Art Deco · 1

  • Frist Art MuseumDowntown
    1930s Art Deco post office turned art museum — the postal counter in the basement still works

🏮 Hidden alleys · 1

  • Printer's AlleyDowntown
    Nashville's oldest nightlife district — a narrow downtown alley that went from publishing hub to Prohibition speakeasies to neon-lit clubs.

🌿 Green & river · 1

  • Radnor Lake State Natural AreaOak Hill
    A railroad cattle lake inside Nashville city limits that became a wildlife sanctuary

🍷 Natural wine · 1

  • The Patterson HouseThe Gulch
    Nashville's pioneering craft-cocktail speakeasy, now reopened on the fifth floor of a 1920s Gulch warehouse after 16 years on Division Street.

💿 Record shops · 1

  • United Record PressingWedgewood-Houston
    The historic Chestnut Street vinyl plant that pressed Motown's hits and the first Beatles single in America — complete with the segregation-era 'Motown Suite' upstairs.
