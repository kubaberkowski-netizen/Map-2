🪦 Death & burial · 6

  • Grave of Just NuisanceRed Hill, Simon's Town
    Granite headstone for the only dog ever formally enlisted in the Royal Navy
  • Gugulethu Seven MemorialGugulethu
    Seven granite figures on a busy Cape Flats roadside mark where police gunned down seven young anti-apartheid activists in 1986.
  • Prestwich MemorialDe Waterkant
    An ossuary built to rebury the thousands of slaves and colonial-era poor whose unmarked graves were dug up by a luxury-flats development.
  • Prestwich Memorial OssuaryDe Waterkant
    Shelves of boxed slave bones dug up by a property developer in 2003
  • Rhodes CottageMuizenberg
    The modest seaside cottage where empire-builder Cecil John Rhodes died in 1902, now a small museum on Muizenberg's Main Road.
  • Tana Baru CemeteryBo-Kaap
    Oldest Muslim burial ground at the Cape, packed with the graves of slave imams

🌿 Green & river · 4

  • Boulders Penguin ColonySimon's Town
    A land-based colony of endangered African penguins that waddles among the granite boulders and back gardens of a Simon's Town beach.
  • Company's GardenCity Bowl
    The VOC's 1652 vegetable plot that fed passing ships, now a shaded city park guarding the oldest cultivated pear tree in the country.
  • Kirstenbosch National Botanical GardenNewlands
    The first botanical garden in the world devoted to a country's own indigenous flora, laid out on land Cecil Rhodes left to the nation — now circled by a snaking treetop walkway nicknamed the Boomslang.
  • Saffron Pear TreeCompany's Garden
    A 360-year-old pear, propped on crutches, planted with the first Dutch settlers

📜 Deep history · 4

  • Castle of Good HopeCity Bowl
    South Africa's oldest surviving colonial building, a five-pointed Dutch East India Company stone fort raised on what was then the shoreline.
  • Het PosthuysMuizenberg
    A whitewashed Dutch East India Company signal post from 1673 that has been a brothel, an ale house and a police station — among the oldest buildings in South Africa.
  • Maclear's BeaconTable Mountain summit
    The highest point of Table Mountain is marked by a black stone cairn built in 1844 to help measure the curvature of the Earth.
  • Mostert's MillMowbray
    A 1796 Dutch tower windmill on the slopes of Devil's Peak — gutted by the 2021 wildfire, ground back to working order by 2024.

⚓ Maritime · 4

  • Green Point LighthouseMouille Point
    A candy-striped tower on the Mouille Point seafront, the oldest working lighthouse in South Africa and a fog-horn neighbours have long grumbled about.
  • Kalk Bay Harbour & the Brass BellKalk Bay
    A working fishing harbour where the day's catch is auctioned off the quay, beside a seafood institution wedged between the railway line and the surf.
  • Mariner's Wharf & the Republic of Hout BayHout Bay
    Africa's first harbourfront emporium, in a fishing village that once cheekily declared itself an independent republic with its own passports.
  • Wreck of the SS KakapoNoordhoek
    The rusting iron skeleton of a steamship that ran full-tilt onto Noordhoek's Long Beach in 1900 after her officer mistook Chapman's Peak for Cape Point.

🗿 Oddities · 4

  • Just Nuisance StatueSimon's Town
    A bronze Great Dane on the harbour overlooks Jubilee Square — the only dog ever officially enlisted in the Royal Navy.
  • Long Street BathsCape Town City Centre
    Edwardian municipal pool with the only public Turkish baths in the country
  • Rondebosch FountainRondebosch
    A cast-iron Victorian drinking fountain stranded on a traffic island, with troughs for horses, basins for dogs and a lantern said to be among Cape Town's first electric lights.
  • The Noon GunSignal Hill
    A pair of Georgian cannon that have boomed midday across the Mother City since 1806, audible from the streets of Bo-Kaap below.

🏛️ Museums · 3

  • District Six MuseumZonnebloem
    A former mission church preserving the memory of the inner-city community that apartheid bulldozers erased.
  • Heart of Cape Town MuseumObservatory
    Inside Groote Schuur Hospital, the very rooms where Christiaan Barnard performed the world's first human heart transplant in 1967, recreated with lifelike tableaux.
  • Rust en VreugdCape Town City Centre
    A VOC fiscal's lavish 1770s mansion, now a quiet gallery of colonial watercolours

🛕 Faith & ritual · 3

  • Lutheran Church, Strand StreetCape Town City Centre
    A warehouse secretly run as a church when only Calvinism was legal at the Cape
  • Palm Tree MosqueCape Town City Centre
    Cape Town's second-oldest mosque hidden inside a Long Street townhouse
  • St George's Cathedral & the Arch for ArchCity Bowl
    Desmond Tutu's 'People's Cathedral', where he led the 1989 march against apartheid and where his ashes now rest beneath the high altar.

🌆 Rooftop views · 2

  • Cape Point Lighthouse & Flying Dutchman FunicularCape of Good Hope
    A clifftop 1859 lighthouse reached by Africa's first commercial funicular, at the storm-lashed headland that gave the world the legend of a ghost ship.
  • Table Mountain Aerial CablewayTafelberg Road, Table Mountain
    Since 1929 a cable car has hauled visitors up Table Mountain's sheer face — and since 1997 its floor rotates a full 360 degrees on the way.

🏊 Lidos & ponds · 2

  • Graaff's PoolSea Point
    A walled tidal pool on the Sea Point promenade built so an invalid wife could bathe unseen, reached by a private tunnel under the road.
  • Muizenberg Beach HutsMuizenberg
    A row of candy-coloured Victorian bathing boxes at Surfer's Corner, descendants of the wheeled 'bathing machines' that preserved Edwardian modesty.

🍷 Natural wine · 2

  • Groot ConstantiaConstantia
    South Africa's oldest wine estate, granted to governor Simon van der Stel in 1685 — the cradle of the country's wine industry, with a gabled Cape Dutch manor and Cloete cellar.
  • Klein ConstantiaConstantia
    The estate behind Vin de Constance, the fabled sweet Constantia wine that Napoleon had shipped to his exile on St Helena and reputedly drank on his deathbed.

🌍 Diaspora London · 1

  • Auwal MosqueBo-Kaap
    The first mosque in South Africa, founded amid the painted houses of Bo-Kaap and tended by an exiled imam who wrote out the Quran from memory in prison.

🏰 Follies · 1

  • Rhodes MemorialDevil's Peak
    A granite Greek temple bolted to the flank of Devil's Peak, with eight bronze lions and 49 steps for the 49 years of Cecil Rhodes's life.
