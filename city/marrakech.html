Offbeat & storied places in Marrakech

📜 Deep history · 7

  • Bab AgnaouKasbah
    Carved Almohad gate into the royal kasbah, eight centuries weathered
  • Bahia PalaceMellah
    A single-storey labyrinth of courtyards built by a grand vizier too heavy for stairs, named for his favourite wife.
  • El Badi PalaceKasbah
    The skeleton of a once-incomparable Saadian palace, stripped to its mud-brick bones and now ruled by nesting storks.
  • Mouassine FountainMouassine
    A 16th-century Saadian wall-fountain with a carved cedar canopy: one basin for people, two for their animals, charity made architecture.
  • Place des FerblantiersMellah
    Tinsmiths' square at the Mellah's edge, ringing with hammers
  • Shrob ou Shouf FountainBen Youssef
    A Saadian street fountain whose name is a standing order to passers-by: 'drink and look', carved palm-wood canopy and all.
  • Theatre RoyalGueliz
    Boccara's domed opera house, half-built and waiting decades on

🏛️ Museums · 6

  • Dar el Bacha (Musee des Confluences)Medina (Bab Doukkala)
    The pasha Glaoui's palace, now a museum and a famous coffee bar
  • Dar Si SaidRiad Zitoun
    A defence minister's painted-cedar palace, now Morocco's national museum of carpets and weaving, around a riad garden pavilion.
  • Maison de la PhotographieBen Youssef
    A restored merchants' inn hung with vintage prints of a vanished Morocco: caravans before trucks, mountain villages before roads.
  • Marrakech Museum (Dar Mnebhi)Ben Youssef
    A war minister's mansion seized by the brutal Pasha Glaoui while its owner was abroad, now a museum under a vast metal chandelier.
  • Musee BoucharouiteAzbest (Ben Youssef)
    A small museum for Berber rag-rugs knotted from worn-out clothes
  • Musee TiskiwinRiad Zitoun
    One Dutch traveller's lifetime hoard of Saharan textiles and jewellery, arranged as a journey down the old caravan road to Timbuktu.

🛕 Faith & ritual · 5

  • Bab Doukkala MosqueBab Doukkala
    Saadian Friday mosque founded by a sultan's formidable mother
  • Ben Youssef MadrasaBen Youssef
    A 16th-century Quranic college where 900 students once crammed into 130 tiny cells around a pool of carved cedar and zellij.
  • Koubba of Lalla ZohraKoutoubia esplanade
    Whitewashed tomb of the woman saint who became a dove, by the Koutoubia
  • Slat al-Azama SynagogueMellah
    The blue-tiled Lazama synagogue, founded by Jews fleeing the Spanish expulsion of 1492 and still tucked inside a Mellah courtyard.
  • Zaouia of Sidi Bel AbbesBab Taghzout
    The shrine of the patron of the blind, greatest of the seven saints whose week-long pilgrimage spirals counter-clockwise around the city.

🌿 Green & river · 4

  • Agdal Gardens (Dar al-Hana basin)South of the Kasbah
    Almohad royal olive groves around a vast pool where a sultan drowned
  • Cyber Park (Arsat Moulay Abdeslam)Medina
    An 18th-century prince's pleasure orchard that the French turned botanical and the telecoms age wired with free internet terminals.
  • Le Jardin SecretMouassine
    Twin riad gardens fed by an ancient underground khettara, derelict for decades and only coaxed back to life in 2016.
  • Menara GardensHivernage
    A green-roofed pavilion mirrored in a vast 12th-century irrigation basin, fringed with olives and the legends of drowned lovers.

🗿 Oddities · 4

  • Bab Debbagh TanneriesBab Debbagh
    The only city gate named for a trade, opening onto stone vats where hides have been cured the same pungent medieval way for 900 years.
  • Bab el-KhemisNorthern medina
    The Thursday Gate, fronting a sprawling junk-and-flea market
  • Dar BellarjMedina (Ben Youssef)
    The House of Storks, a former bird hospital turned arts foundation
  • Rahba KedimaSouks
    The old apothecaries' square where herbalists still sell dried chameleons and roots for love spells alongside the saffron and cumin.

🪦 Death & burial · 3

  • European CemeteryGueliz
    A quiet colonial-era graveyard in the new town where French settlers, Free French soldiers and stray foreign souls of every faith lie together.
  • Miaara Jewish CemeteryMellah
    A vast field of whitewashed tombs on the medina's edge, including a corner for thousands of children lost to a single typhus epidemic.
  • Saadian TombsKasbah
    A Saadian royal necropolis walled up by a rival sultan and forgotten for two centuries, until an aerial survey spotted it from the air in 1917.

🏰 Medieval · 1

  • Almoravid KoubbaBen Youssef
    Marrakech's oldest standing monument: a lone Almoravid ablution pavilion that survived nine centuries by being buried under the street.

📖 Books & writers · 1

  • Dar CherifaMouassine
    Late-16th-century Saadian house, now Marrakech's first literary cafe
